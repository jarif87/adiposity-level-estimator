BodyFat Estimation Tool

Context

Estimates the percentage of body fat for 252 men using underwater weighing and body circumference measurements.

Educational Use

This dataset is ideal for illustrating multiple regression techniques. Accurate body fat measurement is costly, so simpler methods are desirable.

Variables

  1. Density (underwater weighing)
  2. Percent body fat (Siri's equation)
  3. Age (years)
  4. Weight (lbs)
  5. Height (inches)
  6. Neck circumference (cm)
  7. Chest circumference (cm)
  8. Abdomen 2 circumference (cm)
  9. Hip circumference (cm)
  10. Thigh circumference (cm)
  11. Knee circumference (cm)
  12. Ankle circumference (cm)
  13. Biceps (extended) circumference (cm)
  14. Forearm circumference (cm)
  15. Wrist circumference (cm)

Measurements follow standards from Behnke and Wilmore (1974). Abdomen 2 circumference is measured laterally at the iliac crests and anteriorly at the umbilicus.

Siri's Equation

Percentage of Body Fat = 495 / Density - 450

Source

Data provided by Dr. A. Garth Fisher, Human Performance Research Center, Brigham Young University.
